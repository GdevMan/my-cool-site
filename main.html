Top 3 Most Popular Malls in the World

1. The Dubai Mall – Dubai, UAE

Footfall: Over 100 million visitors per year

Why it’s famous: It’s not just a mall — it’s an experience. With over 1,200 shops, an indoor aquarium, an ice rink, and a direct view of the Burj Khalifa, this place defines luxury shopping.

Must-see: The Dubai Fountain show and the Dubai Aquarium & Underwater Zoo.

2. SM Mall of Asia – Manila, Philippines

Footfall: ~60 million visitors annually

Why it’s famous: One of the largest malls in Southeast Asia, located by Manila Bay. It features over 600 stores, an IMAX theater, and even a Ferris wheel.

Must-see: The seaside sunset view and weekend fireworks.

West Edmonton Mall – Alberta, Canada

Footfall: ~30 million visitors a year

Why it’s famous: Once the largest mall in the world, it’s a mini-city. It includes a water park, indoor amusement park, mini-golf, and over 800 stores.

Must-see: Galaxyland amusement park and the world’s largest indoor wave pool.

Bonus Tip: All of these malls are located in cities worth visiting even beyond the shopping. If you're planning your next international adventure, consider building your itinerary around one of these mega-malls — and wear comfy shoes.

Spain on Fire: 14 Wildfires Burn as Heatwave Sparks Emergency Across Southern Europe

Published: August 15, 2025
Source: BBC News

Spain is facing a serious wildfire crisis, with Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez warning that the country is at "extreme risk" of more fires breaking out. As of now, 14 major wildfires are actively burning across the nation. In a public statement on social media platform X (formerly Twitter), Sánchez expressed concern over worsening weather conditions, including intense heat and dry winds, which are expected to make the situation even more dangerous. Southern Europe as a whole is experiencing severe heat, with wildfires also affecting Portugal, Greece, France, and parts of the Balkans. The region is enduring record-high temperatures and a prolonged drought, both of which scientists link to climate change. Firefighters are working around the clock, but the scale and intensity of the fires are making containment difficult. Some communities have already been evacuated, and air quality has deteriorated in affected regions.
