How to Play Chess

Objective:
The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king, meaning the king is under attack and can't escape.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Chessboard:

8x8 grid (64 squares), alternating light and dark squares. Each player starts with 16 pieces:
1 King

1 Queen

2 Rooks

2 Knights

2 Bishops

8 Pawns

White moves first, then players alternate turns.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

How Each Piece Moves:

Pawn (P): Moves 1 square forward. On its first move, can move 2 squares. Captures diagonally.

Rook (R): Moves in straight lines: up, down, left, or right.

Knight (N): Moves in an L-shape: 2 squares in one direction, then 1 square perpendicular. Jumps over pieces.

Bishop (B): Moves diagonally.

Queen (Q): Moves any number of squares in any direction (rook + bishop).

King (K): Moves 1 square in any direction.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Special Moves:

Castling:

King moves 2 squares toward a rook; the rook jumps over the king.

Conditions:

No pieces between king and rook.

King and rook haven’t moved.

King is not in check and doesn't move through or into check.

En Passant:

If a pawn moves 2 squares forward and lands beside your pawn, you can capture it as if it had moved 1 square.

Promotion:

When a pawn reaches the other side (8th rank), it must be promoted (usually to a queen).

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What is Check and Checkmate?

Check: Your king is under attack.

Checkmate: Your king is in check and there’s no legal way to escape.

If your king is in check, you must get out of check on your turn.

Game Ends When:

Checkmate happens.

Stalemate: Player has no legal moves but is not in check → draw.

Draw: Can happen by agreement, repetition, 50-move rule, etc.

-----------

Basic Tips:

Control the center of the board.

Develop your pieces (don’t just move pawns).

Don’t bring your queen out too early.

Keep your king safe — castle early!
